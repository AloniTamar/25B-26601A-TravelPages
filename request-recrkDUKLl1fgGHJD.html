1. Carmel Market (Shuk HaCarmel)

Experience the sights, sounds, and most vibrant market in the city, offering a wide range of fresh produce, spices, and local street food. From souvenirs to fresh fruit, this bustling market has it all.



2. White City Bauhaus Architecture

Take a stroll through the White City, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and marvel at the over 4,000 buildings designed in the Bauhaus style, showcasing Tel Aviv's rich architectural heritage. The clean lines, curved balconies, and functional design make for a fascinating urban landscape.



3. Jaffa Flea Market (Shuk HaPishpeshim)

Get lost in the narrow alleys of Jaffa's flea market, filled with antiques, vintage clothing, and eclectic souvenirs. This bustling market offers a glimpse into the city's rich history and multicultural heritage, with plenty of hidden gems waiting to be discovered.
