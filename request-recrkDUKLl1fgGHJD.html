1. Brandenburg Gate

The Brandenburg Gate is a iconic symbol of Berlin and one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world. This stunning gate was built in the 18th century and has played a significant role in many of the city's most important historical events.



2. Museum Island

Museum Island is a UNESCO World Heritage site that's home to five of Berlin's most important museums, including the Alte Nationalgalerie and the Pergamon Museum. The island is a treasure trove of cultural and artistic riches from around the world.



3. Berlin Wall Memorial

The Berlin Wall Memorial is a poignant reminder of the city's tumultuous history, which divided East and West Berlin during the Cold War era. The memorial features the last remaining section of the wall, as well as a museum and a chapel of reconciliation.
