1. Anne Frank House

The Anne Frank House is a museum dedicated to the Jewish girl who wrote a diary during WWII, hiding from the Nazis in Amsterdam. The museum features a reconstructed annex where Anne and others lived in hiding.



2. Rijksmuseum

The Rijksmuseum is a national museum showcasing Dutch art and history, featuring an impressive collection of works, including Rembrandt's Night Watch. Its grand architecture and beautiful gardens make it a must-visit.



3. Vondelpark

Vondelpark is a beautiful and peaceful green oasis in the city, perfect for a stroll, picnic, or people-watching. This largest park in Amsterdam also hosts various events and festivals throughout the year.
