1. Anne Frank House

The Anne Frank House is a museum dedicated to the Jewish girl who wrote a diary during WWII, hiding from the Nazis in Amsterdam. The museum features a reconstructed annex where Anne and four others lived in secrecy.



2. Rijksmuseum

The Rijksmuseum is a national museum showcasing Dutch art and history, including works by Rembrandt, Vermeer, and other Dutch Masters. The museum's grand architecture and beautiful gardens are also worth a visit.



3. Vondelpark

Vondelpark is a beautiful and peaceful urban park, perfect for a stroll or picnic. The park is home to several sculptures, gardens, and a lovely escape from the bustling city center.
