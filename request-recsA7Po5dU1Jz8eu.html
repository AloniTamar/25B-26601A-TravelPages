1. Anne Frank House

The Anne Frank House is a museum dedicated to the Jewish girl who wrote a diary during WWII, hiding from the Nazis in Amsterdam. The museum features a reconstructed version of the secret annex where Anne wrote her famous diary.



2. Rijksmuseum

The Rijksmuseum is a national museum showcasing Dutch art and history, featuring an impressive collection of masterpieces by Rembrandt, Vermeer, and other famous Dutch Masters. The museum's grand architecture and beautiful gardens are also worth exploring.



3. Vondelpark

Vondelpark is a beautiful and peaceful oasis in the heart of Amsterdam, perfect for a stroll, picnic, or bike ride. This 19th-century park is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, featuring walking paths, ponds, and plenty of scenic spots to relax.
