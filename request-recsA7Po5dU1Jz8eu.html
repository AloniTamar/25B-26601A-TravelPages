1. Anne Frank House

The Anne Frank House is a museum dedicated to the Jewish girl who wrote a diary during WWII, hiding from the Nazis in Amsterdam. The museum features a reconstructed version of the secret annex where Anne Frank and her family lived in hiding.



2. Rijksmuseum

The Rijksmuseum is a national museum showcasing Dutch art and history, including works by Rembrandt, Vermeer, and other famous Dutch Masters. The museum's impressive collection spans from the Middle Ages to the 19th century.



3. Jordaan Neighborhood

The Jordaan Neighborhood is a charming and historic area known for its narrow streets, picturesque canals, and quaint shops and cafes. Visitors can stroll through the neighborhood, admire the architecture, and visit the famous Noordermarkt square, which hosts a bustling outdoor market on Mondays.
