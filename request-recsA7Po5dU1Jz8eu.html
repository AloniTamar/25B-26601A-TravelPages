1. Anne Frank House

The Anne Frank House is a museum dedicated to the Jewish girl who wrote a diary during WWII, hiding from the Nazis in Amsterdam. The museum features a reconstructed version of the secret annex where Anne wrote her diary.



2. Rijksmuseum

The Rijksmuseum is a Dutch national museum showcasing an impressive collection of art and history, including works of Rembrandt, Vermeer, and other famous Dutch Masters. The museum's grand architecture and beautiful gardens are also worth a visit.



3. Vondelpark

Vondelpark is a beautiful and peaceful oasis in the heart of Amsterdam, perfect for a stroll or picnic. This 45-hectare green space is home to several sculptures, playgrounds, and cafes, making it a great spot to relax and enjoy the city's atmosphere.
