1. The Rijksmuseum

This iconic museum is home to an impressive collection of Dutch art and history, featuring works by Rembrandt, Vermeer, and other famous Dutch Masters. The building itself is also a work of art, with stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.



2. The Anne Frank House

This museum is dedicated to the Jewish girl who wrote a diary during WWII, hiding from the Nazis in Amsterdam. The house has been converted into a museum, showcasing the secret annex where Anne and her family lived in hiding.



3. The Vondelpark

This beautiful park is Amsterdam's largest and most popular green spaces, perfect for a relaxing stroll, picnic, or people-watching. With several cafes, ponds, and walking trails, it's a great place to unwind and enjoy the city's laid-back atmosphere.
