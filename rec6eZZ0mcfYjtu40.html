Top travel recommendations for Berlin

Brandenburg Gate

The Brandenburg Gate is Berlin’s most iconic landmark and a symbol of the city’s tumultuous past. This magnificent structure was built in the 18th century as a gate to the city and has played a significant role in many historical events. It is a must-visit attraction in Berlin and offers stunning views of the city. Today, it is a symbol of unity and freedom. Be sure to take a stroll through the adjacent Tiergarten park.

Museum Island

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Museum Island is home to five of Berlin’s most important museums including Alte Nationalgalerie, Altes Museum, Bode Museum, Neues Museum, and Pergamon Museum. The island is a treasure trove of art and culture from around the world. Spend a day exploring the vast collections and admiring the stunning architecture of the museums.

Berlin Wall Memorial

The Berlin Wall Memorial at Bernauer Strasse offers a glimpse into the city’s past. The memorial preserves the last remnants of the Berlin Wall and provides insight into the division of the city during the Cold War era. Visitors can see the last remaining section of the wall, the watchtower, and the memorial to the victims of the wall. This is a poignant reminder of the city’s history and a must-visit attraction.
