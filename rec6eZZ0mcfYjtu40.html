Top travel recommendations for Berlin

Brandenburg Gate

Built in the 18th century, the Brandenburg Gate is one of the most iconic landmarks in Berlin. It is located at the western end of the Unter den Linden avenue, and it is a must-visit attraction for its historical significance and architectural beauty. The gate was once a symbol of the division of the city, but today it is a symbol of unity and freedom. The gate is beautifully lit at night, making it a popular spot for tourists and locals alike.

Museum Island

Museum Island is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in the Spree River. It is home to five of Berlin's most important museums, including the Alte Nationalgalerie, the Altes Museum, the Bode Museum, the Neues Museum, and the Pergamon Museum. These museums house a vast collection of art and artifacts from around the world, including works by Monet, Van Gogh, and Picasso. Visitors can buy a combined ticket to visit all the museums on the island.

Berliner Fernsehturm

The Berliner Fernsehturm, also known as the Berlin TV Tower, is a 368-meter-tall observation tower located in 1969. It was built to commemorate the 20th anniversary of the German Democratic Republic. The tower offers stunning views of Berlin from its observation deck, which is 203 meters high. Visitors can also enjoy a snack or a meal at the rotating restaurant.
