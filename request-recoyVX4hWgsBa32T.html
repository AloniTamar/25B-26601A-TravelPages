1. Carmel Market (Shuk HaCarmel)

This bustling marketplace is a sensory delight, offering a wide range of fresh produce, spices, and souvenirs, as well as restaurants and cafes serving up delicious local cuisine. From Tuesday to Saturday, you can also explore the adjacent Nachalat Binyamin Pedestrian Mall.



2. White City Bauhaus Architecture

Tel Aviv is known as the "White City" due to its stunning collection of over 4,000 Bauhaus buildings, many of which are now UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Take a self-guided walking tour to admire the clean lines, curved balconies, and ornate details of these iconic structures.



3. Tayelet Beach Promenade

This scenic beachfront promenade stretches for over 13 kilometers, offering breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea and the city skyline. Take a leisurely stroll, jog, or bike ride along the Tayelet, stopping at one of the many cafes, restaurants, or beaches along the way.
