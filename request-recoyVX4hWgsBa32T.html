1. Brandenburg Gate

The Brandenburg Gate is an iconic symbol of Berlin, a stunning neoclassical monument that has witnessed the city's turbulent history. Today, it's a popular spot for tourists and locals alike, offering great photo opportunities and a glimpse into Berlin's rich history.



2. Museum Island

Museum Island is a UNESCO World Heritage site that's home to five of Berlin's most important museums, including the Alte Nationalgalerie and the Pergamon Museum. Visitors can explore a vast collection of art and artifacts from around the world, including Egyptian antiquities and European art from the 19th and 20th centuries.



3. Berlin Wall Memorial

The Berlin Wall Memorial is a poignant tribute to the city's turbulent past, offering a glimpse into the lives of those who lived along the Wall and the events that shaped the city's history. Visitors can see the last remaining section of the Wall, the Berlin Wall Memorial at Bernauer Strasse, and the Berlin Wall Documentation Center.
