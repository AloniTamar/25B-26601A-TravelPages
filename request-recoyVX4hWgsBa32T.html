1. Carmel Market (Shuk HaCarmel)

This bustling marketplace is a sensory delight, offering a wide range of fresh produce, spices, and souvenirs. Try some local street food and soak up the lively atmosphere.



2. White City Bauhaus Architecture

Take a stroll through the White City, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and admire the iconic Bauhaus-style buildings, which were built in the 1930s. These sleek, modernist structures are a testament to Tel Aviv's rich cultural heritage.



3. Gordon Beach (Gordon-Frishman Beach)

Relax on one of Tel Aviv's most popular beaches, known for its clean sand, calm waves, and lively atmosphere. Enjoy a refreshing swim, take a leisurely stroll along the promenade, or try your hand at water sports like surfing or paddleboarding.
