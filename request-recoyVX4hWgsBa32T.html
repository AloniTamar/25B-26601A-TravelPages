1. Carmel Market (Shuk HaCarmel)

This vibrant marketplace is a sensory overload of colors, smells, and sounds, offering a wide variety of fresh produce, spices, and local street food. It's a great place to try some authentic Israeli cuisine and mingle with the locals.



2. White City (HaIr HaLevana)

This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a collection of over 4,000 buildings built in the 1930s in the Bauhaus style, which gives Tel Aviv its nickname "The White City." Take a stroll through the streets and admire the unique architecture, which is both functional and aesthetically pleasing.



3. Gordon Beach (Hof Gordon)

This popular beach is one of Tel Aviv's most iconic spots, offering a lively atmosphere, clear waters, and a wide range of amenities like restaurants, bars, and sun loungers. It's a great place to relax, people-watch, and enjoy the city's vibrant energy.
