1. Brandenburg Gate

The Brandenburg Gate is a stunning example of neoclassical architecture, built in the 18th century as a symbol of peace and unity. Today, it's one of Berlin's most iconic landmarks and a popular spot for photos and people-watching.



2. Museum Island

Museum Island is a UNESCO World Heritage site and home to five of Berlin's most important museums, including the Alte Nationalgalerie and the Pergamon Museum.



3. Berlin Wall Memorial

The Berlin Wall Memorial is a poignant tribute to the city's division during the Cold War era. The memorial features a preserved section of the wall, a watchtower, and a museum that tells the stories of those who attempted to cross the border.
