1. Carmel Market (Shuk HaCarmel)

This bustling marketplace offers a sensory experience, with colorful stalls selling fresh produce, spices, and souvenirs, alongside restaurants and cafes serving delicious street food. Explore the alleys and discover unique shops and eateries.



2. White City (HaIr HaLevana)

This UNESCO World Heritage Site features over 4,000 buildings showcasing the iconic Bauhaus architectural style, which Tel Aviv is famous for. Take a guided tour to explore the beautifully restored buildings, many of which now house museums, galleries, and trendy cafes.



3. Gordon Beach (Hof Gordon)

Relax on this vibrant beach, popular with locals and tourists alike. Enjoy the lively atmosphere, take a dip in the Mediterranean, and soak up the sun while enjoying the many food and drink stalls and restaurants nearby.
